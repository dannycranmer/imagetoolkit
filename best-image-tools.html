TinyPNG (also known as TinyJPG) specializes in image compression. It uses smart lossy compression to reduce file sizes of PNG and JPEG images with minimal quality loss. It's fast, simple, and widely used by web developers. Processing happens on their servers.

4 Squoosh — Best for Developers

Squoosh is an open-source image compression app built by the Google Chrome team. It runs in the browser using WebAssembly, offering advanced codec options (MozJPEG, WebP, AVIF, JPEG XL) with real-time previews. It's developer-focused and technically impressive, but limited to a single tool: compression.

Privacy: 100% client-side
Tools: 1 (compress/convert)
Signup: Never required
Price
Free
Open source, fully free

Pros

  • Client-side processing — fully private
  • Advanced codec options (AVIF, JPEG XL, WebP)
  • Real-time side-by-side quality preview
  • Open source (Google Chrome Labs)
  • Works as a PWA / installable

Cons

  • Only one tool — compression/conversion
  • No batch processing
  • Developer-focused UI — intimidating for beginners
  • No resize, crop, watermark, or other editing features
  • Development has slowed significantly
Our verdict: Squoosh is technically excellent for fine-tuning compression settings and comparing codecs. Developers who need precise control over AVIF or WebP output will appreciate it. But it's a single-purpose tool — if you need resizing, cropping, watermarking, or any other image editing, you'll need another tool alongside it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free alternative to iLoveIMG?

PicBrew is the best free alternative to iLoveIMG. It offers 17+ image tools (compress, resize, convert, crop, watermark, and more) with no limits, no ads, and no signup. Unlike iLoveIMG, PicBrew processes everything in your browser — your images are never uploaded to a server.

What is the best free alternative to TinyPNG?

PicBrew's Image Compressor is a free alternative to TinyPNG with no daily limits. TinyPNG limits free users to 20 images per day and 5 MB per file. PicBrew has no such limits, processes images locally in your browser, and also includes 16 other tools beyond compression.

Are my images safe when using online image tools?

It depends on the tool. Most online image tools (iLoveIMG, TinyPNG, Remove.bg, Canva) upload your files to their servers for processing. PicBrew and Squoosh are exceptions — they process everything client-side in your browser, so your images never leave your device.

Which image tool has the most features?

For dedicated image processing tools, PicBrew and iLoveIMG offer the most features (15-17+ tools each). Canva has more features overall but it's a full design platform, not a focused image tool suite. TinyPNG, Squoosh, and Remove.bg are single-purpose tools.

Do I need to create an account to use PicBrew?

No. PicBrew never requires an account, signup, or email. All 17+ tools are available immediately — just open the page and start using them. There are no usage limits, no watermarks, and no premium tiers.

Can PicBrew work offline?

Yes. Since PicBrew processes everything in your browser using JavaScript and Canvas APIs, it works offline after the initial page load. No internet connection is needed for any image processing operation.
